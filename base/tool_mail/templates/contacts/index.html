Creating an XLSX file with Name, Title, and Email Columns

In this guide, we will explain step-by-step how to create an XLSX file with columns for Name, Title, and Email. You can easily follow these instructions to prepare your own file. This will help you organize and manage your contacts efficiently.

Step 1: Open Microsoft Excel or a compatible spreadsheet application

Open Microsoft Excel or any compatible spreadsheet application (such as Google Sheets or LibreOffice Calc) on your computer.

Step 2: Create a new spreadsheet

Create a new blank spreadsheet by clicking "File" > "New" > "Blank Workbook" (or the equivalent in your spreadsheet application).

Step 3: Add column headers

In the first row of the spreadsheet, enter the following column headers:

An Important Note:

You can use custom fields from your spreadsheet to personalize your emails. To do this, simply include the column headers in your email text, and the system will automatically replace the headers with the corresponding data for each recipient.

For example, if your spreadsheet contains fields such as "first_name," "last_name," and "company," you can use them in your email as follows:

"Hello {first_name} {last_name}, I hope everything is going well at {company}."

When the email is sent, the system will replace {first_name}, {last_name}, and {company} with the appropriate information for each recipient, personalizing the message for them.

Please ensure that the column headers in your spreadsheet match the field names you want to use in your email text. Additionally, if your header names contain characters that are not in English, make sure to convert them to English before using them in your email text. However, if you happen to miss any, don't worry – we'll automatically convert them for you 😉

  • • In cell A1, type Name.
  • • In cell B1, type Title.
  • • In cell C1, type Email.

It should look like this:

            
    -------------------------------
    |    A    |    B    |    C    |
    -------------------------------
    |  Name   |  Title  |  Email  |
    -------------------------------

Step 4: Enter contact information

Now, start entering the contact information for each person in the corresponding columns:

  • • In column A, enter the person's name.
  • • In column B, enter the person's title or job position.
  • • In column C, enter the person's email address.

For example:

            
    ------------------------------------------------------
    |       A      |       B      |           C          |
    ------------------------------------------------------
    |     Name     |     Title    |         Email        |
    ------------------------------------------------------
    | Cevdet Ozturk| Manager      | cevdet@sellervibe.co |
    ------------------------------------------------------
    | Yunus H      | Coordinator  | yunush@sellervibe.co |
    ------------------------------------------------------

Continue adding contacts by filling out each row with the relevant information.

Step 5: Save the file as XLSX

Once you've entered all the contact information, save the file in the XLSX format:

  1. 1. Click "File" > "Save As" (or the equivalent in your spreadsheet application).
  2. 2. Choose a location where you want to save the file.
  3. 3. In the "Save as type" dropdown menu (or the equivalent in your application), select "Excel Workbook (*.xlsx)".
  4. 4. Enter a name for the file and click "Save".

Your XLSX file with Name, Title, and Email columns is now ready. Now, upload this file to our platform and start using it to take full advantage of our features.
